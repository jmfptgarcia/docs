Your team is starting to use AI assistants like Claude with your real business
systems — your CRM, your email, your support desk. That's powerful, and it
raises an immediate question every responsible team asks:

**"What exactly is the AI allowed to do — and who decides?"**

MCP Gateway is that decision, made real. It sits between the AI assistant and
your business tools, and enforces the rules you set:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="You choose the actions" icon="list-check">
    Allow reading contacts but not deleting them. Allow drafting emails but
    not sending. Every action is a separate, explicit decision.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Per person, not per company" icon="users">
    Your sales rep and your intern don't get the same powers. Permissions
    follow each person, using the same work login they already have.
  </Card>
  <Card title="New abilities are blocked by default" icon="shield">
    When a connected tool adds new capabilities, they stay switched off
    until you've reviewed and approved them. Nothing sneaks in.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Everything is on the record" icon="clock-rotate-left">
    Every action the AI takes — and every permission change an admin makes —
    lands in one audit trail.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How a trial works

During the pilot phase we set up your workspace for you — no installation on
your side.

1. We create your private workspace and connect it to your systems together
   (a 30-minute call).
2. You decide the rules: which roles exist, who holds them, what each role
   may do. Takes minutes in the console.
3. Your team adds the connector to Claude and works normally. The gateway
   quietly enforces your rules on every single action.
