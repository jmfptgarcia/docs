title: "Getting started (for admins)"
description: "From first sign-in to your team's first governed AI action, in about 15 minutes."
---

This guide is for the person who administers the workspace — typically
whoever owns IT, operations, or the CRM. No technical background needed.

<Note>
During the pilot phase, we create your workspace and send you a sign-in
link. If you don't have yours yet, contact us.
</Note>

## 1. Sign in to your console

Open the console link we sent you and click **Sign in with SSO**. Use your
normal work account (Microsoft or Google) — there is no separate password to
manage.

*[screenshot: console sign-in page]*

## 2. Connect your first system

Open **Integrations** in the sidebar and click **Connect** on the system you
use — for example HubSpot.

You'll be taken to HubSpot's own consent screen (the same kind you've seen
when connecting any two business tools). Approve it, and you're back in the
console.

*[screenshot: integrations page with HubSpot card]*

<Note>
Connecting a system does **not** give the AI any abilities yet. It only lets
the gateway see what abilities *exist*, so you can decide about them.
</Note>

## 3. Review what the AI could do — and decide

Every ability the connected system offers appears in the **Review queue**,
switched off. This is deliberate: nothing works until you've looked at it.

For each item, you'll see what it does in plain terms — for example:

- *List contacts* — reads contact records
- *Delete contact* — **removes** a contact record
- *Send email* — sends on a person's behalf

Click **Approve** on the ones your team should ever be able to use, and
**Keep blocked** on the ones they shouldn't. You can change your mind any
time.

*[screenshot: review queue with pending items]*

## 4. Create roles

Open **Roles**. A role is a job description in permission form — you define
it once and assign it to many people.

A good starter set:

| Role | What it allows |
| --- | --- |
| Read-only | Look things up. Change nothing. |
| Sales rep | Read and update contacts, companies, and deals. No deleting. |
| Ops admin | Everything you approved in step 3. |

Click into a role to set its permissions on the grid — each row is a kind of
record, each column an action (read / create / update / delete). Tick what
the role allows.

*[screenshot: role permission grid]*

## 5. Invite your team

Open **Users** and add each person by their **work email**. Pick their role.
That's the whole form — their name fills in automatically the first time
they sign in.

If one person needs an exception ("like Sales rep, but she may also
delete"), open that person and adjust the grid — exceptions are marked with
a dot so you can always see who deviates from their role, and why the audit
trail says so.

*[screenshot: users page with a person selected]*

## 6. Give your team the connector

Each workspace has a **connector address** (we send yours with the
workspace). Share it with your team along with the
[end-user guide](/guides/end-users) — adding it to Claude takes them about
two minutes, and they sign in with the same work account they always use.

## You're done — here's what you got

From this moment, every single action the AI attempts is checked against
your rules, in real time:

- A sales rep asking Claude to *update a deal* — allowed, done, logged.
- The same rep asking Claude to *delete a contact* — **the delete ability
  isn't even visible to them.** Nothing to misuse.
- Any of it, any time — visible under **Audit**: who, what, when, allowed
  or denied.
